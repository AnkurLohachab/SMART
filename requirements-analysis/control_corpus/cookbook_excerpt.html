How to make a perfect omelette

To make a great omelette you must crack three eggs into a bowl. The chef shall whisk them gently with a fork. The pan must be heated until a drop of water dances across the surface. Cooks should add butter; experienced cooks may add a small amount of cream. The spatula shall be silicone, not metal, to protect the non-stick surface. You must fold the omelette in thirds before sliding it onto the plate.

For a herb omelette, the cook shall finely chop fresh chives. The eggs must be at room temperature. The plate should be warmed in advance.

Bread troubleshooting

If the bread fails to rise, the baker should check whether the yeast is active. The dough must be allowed to rest for at least 90 minutes. Bakers shall avoid drafty kitchens and cold worktops.

Knife care

Knives must be sharpened weekly. The blade shall be wiped immediately after use. Cooks should never put a sharp knife in a dishwasher.
